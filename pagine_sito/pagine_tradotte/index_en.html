What is DISCIPLINE?

Here are some definitions of DISCIPLINE:

ENCYCLOPEDIA:

According to the Treccani Encyclopedia, discipline means "the act of learning, and by reflection the act of teaching"; consequently, it refers to any sector of human knowledge organized in such a way as to facilitate its teaching and learning in educational contexts. These terms are commonly used in school environments. Furthermore, the word "discipline" also came to mean the subject of learning and teaching itself, i.e., the subject matter, from which various disciplines derive: literary, scientific, historical, etc.

PHILOSOPHY:

In the philosophical field, discipline (from the Latin disciplina, from disciplinare, meaning "to teach" or "to educate") refers to a specific field of study, such as metaphysics, logic, ethics, or epistemology. In this context, a discipline represents an organized area of knowledge, studied and explored through a specific method, with the goal of understanding fundamental aspects of reality.

SPORT:

In sports, discipline is often understood as the art of doing what you don't want to do as if you loved it. It represents the individual's ability to follow norms, rules, or behaviors, regardless of personal or environmental conditions, and to maintain them consistently over time. Sports discipline includes a set of rules, principles, and attitudes that promote respect for the rules, teammates, opponents, and coaches. It encourages consistency in training and behavior and contributes to the athlete's personal and athletic growth.

Why is DISCIPLINE so important?

Everyone says that discipline is a quality everyone should have, but no one explains why. In fact, discipline can be seen as a form of love, especially toward oneself. It represents an act of commitment and care, which allows us to achieve our goals and live more fulfilling lives. Discipline is a demonstration of respect and care for oneself. When we commit to following a training plan, a diet, or a study routine, we are choosing to take care of our body and mind. Discipline allows us to give up immediate gratification in order to obtain long-term benefits. This shows love for our future self, whom we want to be healthy, accomplished, and happy. In conclusion, discipline is not simply a rule or constraint, but an act of love that allows us to take care of ourselves, our goals, and our future.
